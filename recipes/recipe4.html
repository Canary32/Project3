MARINATED SCORPIONS

INGREDIENTS

  • 6 scorpions (you can use fresh or dried)
  • 4 cups of water
  • 2 tablespoons of salt
  • 1/4 cup of your favorite hot sauce
  • A dehydrator or an oven
    •
  • If you are using ready-to-eat scorpions, all you have to do is open the bag!
  • If you are using frozen scorpions, follow provided instructions on the package.
  • If you have raised or caught your own scorpions, you will need to euthanize them in the freezer a day ahead of time, then boil for 3-5 minutes to kill any parasites, and drain. The easiest way to boil something small, like scorpions, is to bring water in a small pot to a rolling boil, then place scorpions in a fine mesh stainless steel strainer, and lower into boiling water for 3-5 minutes. Then, all you need to do it lift the strainer out of the boiling water, and rinse the scorpions, still in the strainer, with cool water.
    •

    DIRECTIONS

    1

    In a large pot over high heat, bring the water and salt to a boil. Add the scorpions and boil for about 5 minutes. Drain and rinse under cold water.

    2

    Transfer the stink bugs to a large ziplock bag and add the hot sauce. Seal the bag and shake well to coat. Refrigerate for about 24 hours.

    3

    Preheat your dehydrator or oven to 140Â°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper if using an oven.

    4

    Arrange the stink bugs in a single layer on the dehydrator trays or the baking sheet. Dehydrate for about 8 hours or until dry and crisp.
